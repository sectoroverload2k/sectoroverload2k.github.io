# Competitive Analysis — PazNetworks (paznetworks.com)

Paul's company is the model the user wants to learn from: he lands network/phone/hosting/
server-support contracts with large, multi-location companies. This is a decode of *how*,
based on a full review of paznetworks.com.

## His positioning (verbatim)

- Headline: **"Coast-to-coast · Multi-site operators"**
- Tagline: **"Managed IT, security, and AV for franchise, healthcare, hospitality, and retail."**
- Core line: *"Systems engineers running networks, surveillance, access control, and cloud
  infrastructure for multi-site operators coast-to-coast — under one SLA."*

## The secret, in one sentence

> Paul niches on **multi-site operators**, leads with **"one SLA for all your locations,"**
> de-risks the buy with **named logos + vendor certifications + hard metrics**, gets in the
> door with a **free assessment**, and expands **one location into the whole portfolio** —
> while **vendor partner programs feed him referrals.**

## The five mechanics

1. **Niche on multi-site operators, not "businesses."** Franchises, clinic networks, hotel
   groups, gyms — companies with 5/50/500 locations. One sale replicates across the portfolio.
   That's why the big logos (Disney+, Toyota, Marriott, YMCA, Topgolf) are believable even if
   each started as a single site or event.

2. **"Single SLA across every site" is the wedge.** Multi-site operators hate having a different
   vendor per location. "One throat to choke" is the thing a regional ops director takes a
   meeting for.

3. **Stacked proof that de-risks a big contract:**
   - Named client logos (signal "safe to hire").
   - **Partner/certification badges** — Cisco, Microsoft Azure, AWS, Google Cloud, Control4,
     Axis, Ubiquiti, HID, Ring, Nest, Samsung, Yale, Dormakaba, Bose, Yamaha, Logitech, Sonos.
     These are not decoration — **vendor partner programs are a referral/lead channel and let
     him resell.** This is a big part of "how he gets clients."
   - Specific metrics, not adjectives: *"27% incident reduction across a 45-clinic network in
     6 months," "1,079 concurrent users at ACC 2025 with zero unplanned outages," "700+ on-site
     installations nationwide," "30%+ efficiency increase due to AI," "90%+ compatibility rate."*

4. **Productized recurring revenue + easy buying.** CTAs are contract-oriented and low-friction:
   *"Schedule Your IT Assessment," "Get a plan tailored to your sites," "Talk to a Specialist,"
   "Get a Free Consultation."* He even publishes tiered pricing on the marketing arm
   ($590 / $1,290 / $2,490 per month) to normalize "this is a monthly relationship." The free
   IT assessment is the classic foot-in-the-door.

5. **Owns the full stack of a location.** Network + phones + surveillance + access control + AV
   + cloud. Once he's wiring a building, he captures everything in it. Land-and-expand happens
   **within** a site (more services) and **across** sites (more locations).

## Other signals

- Coverage: 30+ U.S. states, "coast-to-coast."
- Sub-platforms: Carolina AI, Carolina CRM, Carolina EMR, Carolina Marketing Pro
  (productized add-ons = more cross-sell surface).
- Support model: 24/7 NOC, single SLA, multi-channel (phone/email/chat/on-site/portal).
- Contact: +1 704-236-1454, info@paznetworks.com, support portal.

## What we copy vs. what we beat

- **Copy:** single front door + niche wedge + free assessment + real proof (logos, certs,
  metrics) + recurring revenue + land-and-expand.
- **Beat him on two axes he can't match:**
  1. **Two-dimensional land-and-expand** — more locations *and* more service lines along the
     entire money-flow of a business (payments → POS/network → cloud/email → site/AI).
  2. **A founder-led content/SEO engine** (anthonyl.com) — Paul has one domain and no content
     engine, so he can't capture inbound search demand. We can.
