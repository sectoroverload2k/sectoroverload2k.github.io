# Brand Architecture — Hub & Spoke

## Current state (what exists today)

| Property | Positioning today | Issue |
|---|---|---|
| choosetriton.com | "Unlock your Tech Potential / Your Consulting Partner" — reads as a **cloud consultancy** | Supposed to be the umbrella, but sells the same thing as the Agency |
| tritonagency.ai | "AI agents that build, deploy & scale" — AI **+ cloud, DevOps, custom dev, security** | Overlaps heavily with the hub and cloud line |
| tritonprocessing.com | "Save 40% on payment processing" — ISO of NAB / Wells Fargo / BMO | Solid wedge; under-used as a network lead source |
| anthonyl.com | personal brand / résumé + tutorials | Authority asset, not yet wired to feed the commercial sites |

## The two problems this architecture fixes

1. **The umbrella isn't an umbrella.** ChooseTriton is positioned as just another cloud
   consultancy instead of the single front door that captures everything and routes it
   (Paul's model). Fix: reposition ChooseTriton to sell the *outcome* and route to spokes.

2. **ChooseTriton and Triton Agency cannibalize each other.** Both sell cloud / DevOps /
   Kubernetes / custom dev / consulting. Buyers can't tell which to hire, and Google can't
   either — that's **keyword cannibalization**, where two of your own pages compete for the
   same queries and both rank worse. Fix: **narrow Triton Agency to AI-only**; let the hub own
   "technology partner" and route cloud/infra to the appropriate line.

## Target model

```
                    anthonyl.com   (authority + SEO engine, the human)
                         │
                         ▼
                  choosetriton.com   (umbrella hub — sells the outcome, routes everything)
   ┌──────────────┬──────────────┬───────────────┬────────────────┬──────────────┐
   ▼              ▼              ▼               ▼                ▼
 Processing     AI/Dev         Cloud/Infra     Network/Phone/    Email/SaaS
 (payments)     (AI only)      (AWS)           Hosting=PazNet     (Triton Email…)
```

The hub sells: *"One partner for payments, technology, and infrastructure — across every
location,"* then routes each need to the right spoke, **including handing network/phone/
hosting/server work to PazNetworks as a named partner.** Paul becomes a *service line you can
sell*, not a competitor.

## Why this hand beats Paul's: two-dimensional land-and-expand

Paul expands in one dimension (more locations). Triton expands in **two** — more locations
*and* more service lines along the money-flow of a business:

> A restaurant needs to **take payments** (Processing) → which needs a **POS + network + wiring**
> (PazNetworks) → running on **cloud infra + email** (Anthony/Triton) → with a **website / AI
> ordering bot** (Triton Agency).

**This is literally the Carolina Brew Supply story from Anthony's own résumé** — he did the POS,
the e-commerce, the Triton processing integration, the AWS infrastructure, SQS print automation,
and the DoorDash/Google Maps delivery module for one business. That is the flagship case study
that proves the entire umbrella. Lead with it.

## The lead-origination edge: payments as the wedge

Payment processing is a *better* foot-in-the-door than Paul's free IT assessment:
- Every business that takes money has a processor, and most overpay.
- "Cut your processing fees 40%+" is an easy yes — pure cost savings, low risk, fast quote.
- It's sticky recurring revenue.

So the funnel runs **backward** from Paul's: win the merchant on processing → expand into
POS/network (**hand to Paul → you sourced the lead**) → expand into cloud/email/AI (you).
Combined with Anthony's content engine, this gives Triton two demand channels Paul lacks:
**cost-savings outreach** and **founder-led SEO** — making Anthony a net *contributor* of leads
to the partnership, not just a recipient.

## Credibility: replace placeholder proof with real proof

The single place Triton is currently *behind* Paul.

- **Remove** generic/invented logos ("Treva, Nirastate, Circle…") and unbelievable claims
  ("100% success rate"), and the literal `0+` placeholder metrics on tritonagency.ai — these
  *lower* trust with sophisticated buyers.
- **Feature the real, strong proof you already own:**
  - Bank/ISO partnerships — *"Registered ISO of North American Bancard; Wells Fargo, BMO Harris,
    Citizens Bank, The Bancorp Bank, FFB Bank."* This is Triton's version of Paul's vendor badges.
  - **Become an AWS Partner (APN)** — co-sell + referral channel, and a badge buyers trust.
  - Real case studies with specific (even modest) metrics: Carolina Brew Supply, WelcomeSign,
    Triton Email.
  - Name the **PazNetworks partnership** — Paul's real logos rub off, and reciprocally.
